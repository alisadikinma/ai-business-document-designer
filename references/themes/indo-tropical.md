## Identity & Mood

A modern Indonesian visual identity that pulls from regional heritage without falling into airport-souvenir clichés. The reference points are Cocoon (Jakarta agency), Aksara Pala packaging, modern Kompas magazine layouts, and the 2024 Tokopedia brand refresh — all of which read as confidently Indonesian without resorting to wayang-and-batik-pattern wallpaper. Warm earth palette grounded in Java jungle green, Garuda red, and Sriwijaya gold. Mood signals: hangat, percaya diri, lokal-tapi-modern, akar yang kuat. The danger this theme defends against is pan-Asian generic — a red-and-gold palette without Indonesian context reads as Chinese New Year retail, not Indonesian heritage.

Palette discipline: pick ONE primary (green OR red) per project, not both. Gold is the bridge accent that works with either primary.

## Typography

- **Display:** Saira Condensed 700 Bold — condensed sans-serif at 36-72pt, modern but with enough character to feel local rather than imported. Free on Google Fonts. Alternate display: a custom batik-inspired or wayang-inspired display face IS encouraged if the project budget allows commissioning; always pair with a FOIT-resistant web fallback like Saira Condensed.
- **Body:** Inter 400 Regular or Manrope 400 Regular at 10-11pt with 1.55 line-height — neutral grotesk that lets the heritage display do the cultural lifting.
- **Accent (labels):** Manrope 600 SemiBold tracked-uppercase at 10-12pt with +120 tracking for section labels in Bahasa Indonesia.

Pairing rule: ONE condensed display + ONE neutral grotesk body. Two families maximum. If the project commissions a custom heritage display face, that REPLACES Saira Condensed; do not stack three.

## Illustration / Image Style

Batik motif used as low-opacity (5-12%) background texture, never as foreground pattern wallpaper. Wayang kulit silhouettes as silhouette graphics for chapter openers, rendered in single-color (gold or brown) flat shapes. Tropical foliage as photography or illustration — banana leaf, daun pisang, kelapa, pakis — shot or drawn with depth and grain rather than flat-vector cliché. Photography emphasis: Indonesian artisanal craft in process (hand pouring kopi, tangan menenun, dapur mengulek sambal), market scenes from pasar tradisional, rural-modern context (sawah dengan motor matic, warung kopi dengan WiFi sign), drone shots of sawah terasering. Reference anchor for NB2 prompts: "Indonesian artisanal scene, warm afternoon natural light, hands of a craftsperson in mid-action working on traditional craft (batik tulis canting OR weaving tenun OR pouring kopi tubruk), warm wood and natural textile background, depth-of-field on the hands, warm color grade with green and gold highlights, documentary editorial style, no faces shown, authentic regional context (not Bali resort), shot like Kompas or Tempo photo essay."

## Layout Grammar

- **Grid:** 12-column screen grid or 8-column print grid, 24px gutter, 64px outer margin. Comfortable spacing; the theme breathes rather than crams.
- **Spacing scale:** 8pt baseline — 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128.
- **Heading scale (print A4):** Cover display 64pt, H1 36pt, H2 22pt, H3 14pt (tracked small-caps in Bahasa), body 10pt, caption 8pt.
- **Heading scale (screen):** display 64pt, H1 40pt, H2 24pt, body 16pt.
- **Rules:** gold hairlines at 0.5pt OR a single batik-derived geometric divider motif at section breaks. Never both at the same time.
- **Language convention:** Bahasa Indonesia primary headline at full display weight; optional English secondary line below at half-size in muted color when bilingual is enabled.

## Anti-AI-Slop Banlist for This Theme

1. Lazy "Indonesian tourism" stock photography — Bali sunset at Tanah Lot, Borobudur cliché silhouette, hand holding kelapa muda on a beach — banned. Use authentic regional motifs from beyond Bali (Padang rumah gadang, Yogya keraton, Toraja tongkonan, Manado tinutuan kitchen scene, Riau Melayu motif, sawah Bedugul context only if relevant to the brand origin).
2. Generic pan-Asian-fusion aesthetic — red lanterns, golden dragon motifs, "oriental" ornaments — banned absolutely. This theme is specifically Indonesian, not pan-Asian.
3. Red-and-gold straight Chinese New Year palette without Indonesian context — banned. If red is the primary, it must be paired with Bahasa Indonesia copy, Indonesian motif (batik/wayang/songket NOT dragon-phoenix), and a clearly Indonesian referent in the imagery.
4. Batik used as full-tile wallpaper pattern at 100% opacity — banned. Batik motif must read as subtle texture (5-12% opacity overlay) OR as a single bold motif element used as a graphic device, not a decorative repeating background.
5. Wayang kulit rendered as cute cartoon character — banned. Wayang is silhouette-only, single-color flat fill, treated with cultural respect not as kawaii character design.
