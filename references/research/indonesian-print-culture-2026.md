# 2024-2026 Reference Guide: Indonesian Print Industry Culture and Conventions

## 1. Paper Stock Standard Lokal: Technical Specifications and Use Cases

In the Indonesian *percetakan* (printing) landscape, navigating paper selection requires a balance between aesthetic impact and *ongkos cetak* (production cost). The dominant choice for commercial excellence is the "Art" series, specifically premium brands like **Golden Coin Luxe**, which provide the necessary surface tension for high-fidelity ink laydown.

### Technical Comparison: Art Paper, Art Carton, and Specialty Stocks

| Category | Grammage (gsm) | Texture/Coating | Ink Absorption | Typical Use Cases | Writability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Art Paper** | 85 – 150 | Coated, Smooth, Glossy/Licin | Low (Vibrant colors) | Flyers, Brochures, Calendars, Magazine Inserts | Poor |
| **Art Carton** | 190 – 400 | Coated, Stiff, Semi-Glossy | Low (High contrast) | Business Cards, Company Profiles, Folders | Poor |
| **Ivory** | 210 – 310 | Sisi Glossy / Sisi Matte | Medium (Mixed) | Product Tags, Premium Packaging | One side suitable |
| **HVS** | 80 – 100 | Uncoated, Rough | High (Better for text) | Invoices (Nota), Internal Forms, Books | Excellent |

**Consultant Tip: The Ivory Advantage**
Unlike Art Carton, which is coated on both sides, **Ivory** typically features one glossy side and one matte/uncoated side. For B2B product tags, this is superior; the glossy front carries the brand imagery, while the matte back allows for stamps, price markings, or handwritten notes.

**Finishing: Laminasi Doff vs. Glossy**
*   **Laminasi Glossy:** High-contrast, shiny finish. Maximizes color "pop" but is prone to fingerprints. Use for high-energy retail promos.
*   **Laminasi Doff (Matte):** The "Premium" standard. Beyond the elegant look, I recommend Doff for the **Sawit (Palm Oil)** and **Construction** sectors. It provides essential scratch resistance and protects against the high humidity common in plantation field offices.

**Price Framing (A3+ Digital Print)**
Standard rates range from **Rp 3.100 to Rp 14.200** per sheet. The **Rp 14.200** rate is strictly for "one-off" proofing or *Super Kilat* (3-hour) express services. To hit the **Rp 3.100** economic floor, you must move beyond digital to high-volume offset or *Cetak Cabutan* (gang-run) offset, typically at the 2,000+ unit threshold.

## 2. Printer SOP: Production Logistics and File Specifications

### Regional Profiles & Free Trade Zones (FTZ)
*   **Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung:** High-capacity hubs for industrial offset and 24-hour digital production.
*   **Batam (FTZ):** Operating under a Free Trade Zone status, Batam offers tax-free material imports. However, shipping finished goods out of Batam to other Indonesian regions (DPIL) requires the **PPFTZ-01** document. Per **PMK 62/2012**, this document is critical for the legal release of PPN facilities—failing to provide this will result in cargo being held at customs.

### Turnaround Times (Durasi Kerja)
*   **Super Kilat:** 3 hours (Digital only).
*   **Kilat:** 24 hours.
*   **Hemat/Super Hemat:** 3–4 days (Standard for high-volume *Offset*).

### File Submission Requirements (Mandatory)
*   **Color Mode:** CMYK (RGB files will cause unpredictable color shifts).
*   **Bleed & Margin:** 3mm minimum safety zone.
*   **Formats:** PDF/X-4 (Standard), high-res JPG, or ZIP/RAR archives.
*   **Font Outlining:** You **must** outline all fonts (Ctrl+Shift+O in Illustrator) to prevent the *percetakan* from substituting your brand typeface.

## 3. Bilingual Brochure Conventions: Linguistic Hierarchy

In the Indonesian B2B sector, the "Multilevel DTP" approach is the gold standard for bilingual layouts.

1.  **Indonesian Primary + English Subtitle:** The default for local professionalism. For legal and compliance reasons, the **Bahasa Indonesia layer must be the "Master" layer**, with English translations as a sub-layer in a lighter font weight or italicized.
2.  **English-First:** Reserved for export-oriented manufacturing or multinational firms targeting foreign investors.
3.  **Hokkien/Mandarin Nuance:** For B2B dealings in Medan, Surabaya, and Batam, linguistic choices reflect owner-archetypes. Using direct, no-nonsense language that signals a *Cengli* (fair/reliable) business ethos is more effective than flowery corporate jargon.

## 4. Regulatory Disclaimer: Legal and Tax Compliance (PPN 2025)

Effective **January 1, 2025**, per **PMK 131/2024**, the PPN (VAT) rate is **12%**.

*   **Non-Luxury Goods (General Print):** The effective burden remains at 11% through the **11/12 of Sale Price** formula to determine the *Dasar Pengenaan Pajak* (DPP).
*   **Luxury Goods (Specialty B2B Assets):** For items like high-end property brochures or luxury vehicle collateral:
    *   **January 2025:** Uses the 11/12 formula.
    *   **February 1, 2025 onwards:** The full **12% PPN** applies to the total sale price.

**Mandatory Identifiers:**
*   **NPWP:** Required for all PT/CV entities to generate *Faktur Pajak*.
*   **Certifications:** Place high-res **BPOM** (Consumer goods), **Sertifikat Halal** (F&B), and **ISO** (Manufacturing) logos in the footer or back panel.

## 5. Indonesian B2B Print Culture: Visual Psychology and Owner Archetypes

### Cultural Color Map
*   **Dark Navy + Gold:** Perceived as "Nordic Luxury" (referencing the **Ubuy/Nordic** trend). It signals elite positioning and stability.
*   **Red + Gold:** The "Prosperity" palette. Essential for B2B trading and traditional retail to signal *Heng* (luck) and success.
*   **Green:** Mandatory for Halal-certified products or organic/environmental initiatives.
*   **Red + White:** Signals Nationalist pride; the default for *Instansi Pemerintah* (Government agencies).

### Industry Archetypes
*   **Logistics & Construction:** Prioritize "Trust" via heavy, structural geometric shapes and bold monograms.
*   **Sawit (Palm Oil):** Focus on durability. These owners value "No-Nonsense" layouts that emphasize technical specs and compliance over purely artistic flourishes.

## 6. Headline Convention: Linguistic Tones

*   **B2B Owner-Tone (Direct & Technical):** Focus on *Hitung-hitungan IDR* (cost-efficiency) and jargon-heavy specs. Headlines must prove expertise.
    *   *Example:* "Optimalisasi Rendemen dengan Teknologi Press Terbaru."
*   **B2C Consumer-Tone (Aspirational & Simple):** Follow the **Asmara** agency style—emotive, family-centric, and "short-and-attractive."
    *   *Example:* "Kesegaran Alami untuk Momen Hangat Keluarga."
*   **Colloquial B2B (Hokkien-Flavored):** Used in workshops/logistics. It is direct and prioritizes the *Cengli* (reliable) nature of the deal.

## 7. Agency Style References: The Indonesian Aesthetic

When choosing a design direction, use these benchmarks:
*   **Cocoon Style:** Modern, clean, and brand-centered. Benchmarked for "Digital Transformation" and high-end B2B corporate print.
*   **Asmara Style:** Social-media friendly, emulating the "Social Foundation" aesthetic. Best for B2C connection and vibrant consumer marketing.
*   **Fullstop Indonesia Strategy:** A clear divide—B2B must be **informative and expert-led** (detailed brochures/white papers), while B2C must be **attractive and viral-oriented** (vibrant flyers/visual-heavy posters).

## 8. Indonesia-Print Readiness Checklist

- [ ] **Color Check:** All assets converted to CMYK (No RGB).
- [ ] **PPN Calculation:** Verify the 11/12 DPP formula for 12% PPN (11% effective rate) for non-luxury print.
- [ ] **Luxury Transition:** If printing luxury assets after Feb 1, 2025, apply the full 12% PPN.
- [ ] **Logo Placement:** High-res BPOM, Halal, or ISO logos verified.
- [ ] **Legal Verification:** NPWP and official PT/CV name included on purchase orders.
- [ ] **Batam Protocol:** PPFTZ-01 documentation prepared for PPN facility release (per PMK 62/2012).
- [ ] **Typeface Safety:** All fonts outlined (**Ctrl+Shift+O**) to prevent substitution at the *percetakan*.
- [ ] **Bleed & Trim:** 3mm bleed and internal margin safety check.
- [ ] **Stock Matching:** Grammage matches use case (e.g., Ivory for product tags, Golden Coin Luxe 150gsm for mass flyers).
