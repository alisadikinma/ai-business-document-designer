# Trifold Leaflet — Six-Panel Fold-Aware Framework

## Purpose & When To Use This Framework

Use this framework when the operator needs a 6-panel trifold leaflet — printed on a single A3 sheet folded into thirds, producing 6 panels (3 on each side). Common use cases: museum exhibit guides, conference handouts, lobby leave-behinds, event programs, compact product announcements, NGO awareness pieces.

Per the framework-structures-2026 research backbone (§6 Trifold Leaflet framework), the trifold is a sequence-dependent tool requiring "Fold-line Aware Design" to maintain the kinetic movement of the eye. The reader unfolds the leaflet in a specific order: Front Panel → Inside Flap (the first panel seen upon opening) → Core Spread (three inner panels read left-to-right) → Back Panel (when leaflet is fully unfolded and flipped). Content cannot bleed across fold lines without becoming illegible.

Page count is fixed at 6. This is not a flexible framework — the medium dictates the panel count.

## Narrative Arc / Page Sequence

1. **Panel 1 — Front Panel (Cover / The Hook).** The standalone "pub test" panel: it must communicate the leaflet's purpose without unfolding. Hero headline + dominant visual. Headline is a hook: question, claim, or stake. This is the panel that sits on a lobby table competing with 10 other leaflets — it must earn the unfold.
2. **Panel 2 — Inside Flap (Strategic Enticement).** The first panel seen when the leaflet is opened halfway. Per the research §6: this panel must entice the reader into the Core Spread; it bears the intro / promise / set-up. Do not load it with dry data — that belongs in the Core Spread. Inside Flap is a transition between hook and detail.
3. **Panel 3 — Inner Panel 1 (Problem / Why) — Core Spread Position 1.** Left panel of the fully-opened spread. Articulate the problem, the why-this-matters, or the context the reader needs before the solution lands.
4. **Panel 4 — Inner Panel 2 (Solution / How) — Core Spread Position 2.** Center panel of the fully-opened spread. The solution reveal, the methodology, the product/service core. This is the visual and narrative center of the leaflet.
5. **Panel 5 — Inner Panel 3 (Proof / ROI) — Core Spread Position 3.** Right panel of the fully-opened spread. Evidence: testimonials, measured outcomes, customer logos, case data, or named results. This panel earns the back-panel CTA by establishing credibility.
6. **Panel 6 — Back Panel (Contact + CTA / The Closer).** When the leaflet is fully unfolded and flipped, the back panel carries the contact block + CTA + trust markers. Named contact (phone, WhatsApp, email, address if relevant), one CTA verb, QR code to landing page or scheduling tool, regulatory disclosure if applicable.

The reading sequence — Panel 1 → 2 → 3-4-5 spread → 6 — is dictated by the physical fold. Design must respect this sequence: do not put critical text on a fold line; do not assume the reader reads panels in numeric order without considering the unfold mechanic.

## 7-Step Content Checklist

1. **Front Panel passes the standalone "pub test."** Hand the closed leaflet to someone for 3 seconds. Without unfolding, they must say what it is about. If they cannot, the front panel has failed. Per the research §6: captivation panel is checklist item 1.
2. **Inside Flap entices, does not data-dump.** The Inside Flap is the strategic enticement zone. Use it for promise, intro, or the question that the Core Spread will answer. Per research §6 do-not pattern: overloading the Inside Flap with dry data is a dominant failure.
3. **Critical text never crosses a fold line.** Per research §6 do-not pattern #1: placing critical text on fold lines causes the text to break at the fold crease, becoming illegible. Use the 1.83-inch (≈4.6cm) vertical fold-line guides per research §6 checklist item 6.
4. **Core Spread reads left-to-right as one continuous spread.** When fully opened, the three inner panels (3-4-5) form a continuous visual surface. Design with awareness that the spread is read as a single composition, even though it folds into three.
5. **Visual hierarchy — Center panel (4) carries the strongest visual.** The center panel of the Core Spread is the natural focal point when the leaflet is fully opened. Place the solution / hero visual there. Side panels (3 and 5) scaffold around it.
6. **Gutter margin safety — minimum 6mm from each fold line.** Per research §6 checklist item 7. Text and critical visuals stay at least 6mm from every fold crease. Background color or imagery may bleed across the fold.
7. **Back Panel single CTA + contact block dominates.** When the reader has unfolded and flipped, they are ready to act. One CTA verb. Contact block sized for action (phone number ≥ 2× body copy, per service-flyer convention).

## Do-NOT Patterns (3+)

1. **Placing critical text on fold lines.** Per research §6 do-not #1. Text gets bisected at the crease and becomes unreadable. Use the 1.83-inch fold guides (or whatever guide is generated by the print template — verify with the printer's bleed/fold spec).
2. **Ignoring the 1.83-inch guide logic.** The trifold A3 fold geometry is not arbitrary — it produces panels of specific widths (roughly 9.9cm wide on a 297mm A3 short edge). Design assuming all panels are equal width and respecting the geometry. Per research §6 do-not #2.
3. **Overloading the Inside Flap with dry data.** Per research §6 do-not #3. The Inside Flap is the strategic enticement, not the spec sheet. Spec lives in the Core Spread.
4. **Front Panel that fails the pub test.** A front panel that requires the leaflet to be unfolded to make sense fails the standalone test. The hook must work as a closed leaflet.
5. **Treating the Core Spread as three separate pages.** Designing panels 3, 4, 5 as three independent compositions misses the continuous-spread reading experience. When unfolded, the eye sweeps across; design with that sweep in mind.
6. **Multiple CTA verbs on the Back Panel.** Same single-CTA rule as service-flyer. The Back Panel is the conversion moment; one verb, one destination.
7. **Generic stock imagery on the Front Panel.** Front panel is the standalone test — generic stock fails the test instantly. Use commissioned illustration, location photography, or product/event-specific imagery.

## Anti-Slop Hard Rules

1. **Critical text minimum 6mm from every fold line.** Hard rule. Validate-fail on any headline, CTA, contact info, or critical body copy within 6mm of a fold crease.
2. **Front Panel passes the standalone pub test.** Hard rule. Pre-validation test: hand the closed leaflet to a stranger for 3 seconds; if they cannot say what it is about, redesign.
3. **One CTA verb on Back Panel.** Hard rule.
4. **Visual ratio ≥ 70% across all six panels.** Hard rule.
5. **Core Spread (Panels 3-4-5) designed as a continuous composition.** Hard rule. Background, color field, or unifying visual element must span the spread (with respect for fold gutters).
6. **Fixed 6-panel structure — no expansion, no compression.** Trifold is fixed-format by definition. If 6 panels are insufficient, use a different framework (brochure-product for richer narrative, service-flyer for compressed).
7. **No banned vocabulary.** Global ban list applies. Additional trifold-leaflet bans: "Welcome to our brochure", "In this leaflet you will find", "Thank you for reading" — replace with direct narrative continuation per panel sequence.
