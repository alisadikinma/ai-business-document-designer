# Brochure Product — 7-Page A4 Archetype (INDUSIA Pattern)

## Purpose & When To Use This Framework

Use this framework when the operator needs a print-ready A4 product brochure for high-ticket B2B distribution — sales handover, trade show give-away, lobby leave-behind, or proposal attachment. The brochure is a self-contained sales artifact: the buyer can read it without the salesperson present and walk away knowing what the product does, why it matters, what it costs, and how to buy. Visual ratio ≥ 70% per page.

This is the "INDUSIA archetype" — named after the biological *indusium* (fern spore protective layer) and the INDUSIA AI brochure pattern that crystallized it: a hero claim that protects the product narrative until the reader is ready, then a modular reveal of feature pinnules, then a pricing tier articulation, then a CTA closer. Per the framework-structures-2026 research backbone, this 7-page mandate (with 5-page minimum and 10-page maximum) is the dominant pattern for high-ticket complex sales in 2024–2026.

Default page count is 7. Compress to 5 when the product story is simple (one module, one price). Expand to 10 when there are 4+ modules or a regulatory disclaimer is required.

## Narrative Arc / Page Sequence

1. **Page 1 — Hero Claim ("The Handshake").** Minimalist tagline + high-impact visual. The tagline is a road-sign question or stake-in-the-ground claim. INDUSIA example: "BERAPA TRUK BERANGKAT TANPA ANDA TAHU?" Visual is 70%+ of the page — a single dominant image with information scaffolding around it. No multi-bullet feature list on page 1.
2. **Page 2 — Problem Articulation ("The Friction") [optional but recommended].** The "hitches in the mind" page. Three to five named pain points the buyer recognizes from their daily operations. Visual: pain-illustration or quote from named operator. Sub-text ≤ 25 words per pain point.
3. **Page 3 — Solution Reveal ("Shedding the Indusium").** Transition from problem to core solution. One-line value proposition + the one image that shows the transformation. This is where the brochure stops being a problem catalog and starts being a product reveal.
4. **Pages 4–5 — Modular Feature Catalog ("The Engine").** Each page presents 1–3 product modules ("pinnules"). Per module: module name, what it does (≤ 25 words), one screenshot or product photo, one outcome bullet (measured if possible). Visual ≥ 70%, text scaffolds around the visual. Modules group into 3 pillars maximum (cognitive limit).
5. **Page 6 — Pricing Tier ("The Value").** Bronze / Silver / Gold tier table (or equivalent two-tier or four-tier when justified). Each tier: tier name, price in IDR (Rp + jt/rb shorthand), 4–6 feature checkmarks, single CTA per tier. Middle tier is the "BEST VALUE" anchor — use center-stage highlighting per `references/research/business-model-patterns-2026.md §2`. Display original price strikethrough + discount price using the Accurate-style dual-price slash. PPN 11% disclosure mandatory if Indonesian market.
6. **Page 7 — CTA + Sales Contact ("The Closer").** Named sales contact (face photo, name, role, WhatsApp/phone, email), QR code linking to interactive pricing calculator or product demo, regulatory disclaimer if required (BPOM / Halal / SNI), trust markers (money-back guarantee, integration timeline guarantee, customer count).

## 7-Step Content Checklist

1. **Hero headline ≤ 10 words, road-sign legible.** Test: a reader at 1.5 meters distance must be able to read the headline in under 2 seconds. INDUSIA's "BERAPA TRUK BERANGKAT TANPA ANDA TAHU?" passes; "Comprehensive Fleet Management Solution Platform" fails.
2. **Problem articulation in operator vocabulary.** Use the buyer's words, not the seller's. "Kencing solar" beats "fuel theft incidents." "Truk berangkat tanpa surat jalan" beats "logistics dispatch inefficiency." Pull vocabulary from `references/research/indonesian-print-culture-2026.md` (Indonesian convention) when relevant.
3. **Modular feature pages limit to 3 pillars maximum.** If the product has 5 modules, group them into 3 families. Each module page: module name + outcome (≤ 25 words) + one visual + one measured outcome bullet. No paragraph-of-features.
4. **Pricing tier uses dual-price slash + PPN 11% disclosure.** Per the Accurate benchmark: ~~Rp 4.262.400~~ → **Rp 3.219.000 (Diskon 25%)**. Below the tier table: "Harga sudah termasuk PPN 11%." Tier names: Bronze / Silver / Gold (or equivalent escalation). Middle tier badged "BEST VALUE" or "POPULER."
5. **CTA precision — single CTA per tier.** Each tier has one and only one CTA verb. Options per `references/research/business-model-patterns-2026.md §7`: "PILIH PAKET" (selection), "KONSULTASI GRATIS" (high-touch), "FREE TRIAL 30 HARI" (zero-friction). No dual CTAs ("Buy or Call").
6. **Sales contact is a named human with face photo.** Generic "info@company.com" reads as low-trust. Named contact with photo + WhatsApp + role lifts response rate measurably (see Indonesian B2B research backbone).
7. **Trust markers earn placement on closer page only.** Customer logos, money-back guarantee, integration timeline guarantee, named press wins. No trust marker on hero page — it competes with the road-sign headline.

## Do-NOT Patterns (3+)

1. **Hero page with 7-bullet feature list.** The hero page does one job: place the product on the right shelf in under 2 seconds. A bullet list signals "we do not know what to say first" and burns the only chance at first-impression conversion.
2. **Stock photography of handshakes, hexagon grids, or purple-blue gradient backgrounds.** All three are AI-slop / corporate-slop signals that read "generic" to a B2B operator. Use product-actual photography or commissioned illustration with brand-specific style.
3. **Pricing tier without anchoring or PPN disclosure.** A tier table that shows only "Rp 1.5jt / Rp 3jt / Rp 5jt" without strikethrough anchor pricing leaves the buyer to compare blind. Per the dual-price-slash pattern in research, anchored pricing lifts perceived value.
4. **"Comprehensive end-to-end solution" / "Holistic integrated platform" anywhere.** Banned phrases. Operators read these as filler. Use module names ("POS module + Wristband + Control Room") and named outcomes ("reduce shrinkage 8% per pilot").
5. **CTA proliferation — more than one CTA verb per page.** Each page should have at most one CTA destination. Pricing page has one CTA per tier (so 3 CTAs total, but each tier-scoped). Closer page has one CTA verb directed at the named sales contact.
6. **"Bicycle Bear" interactive mimicry.** Print is fixed-format. Do not pretend the brochure has hover states, expandable panels, or scroll-triggered animations. Per the framework-structures-2026 research §1, fixed-medium designs that mimic edgeless digital interfaces produce fragile artifacts.

## Anti-Slop Hard Rules

1. **Visual ratio ≥ 70% per page.** Hard-fail any page under 60%. This is the dominant rule for brochure-product. If a page is text-heavy, redesign with image-led grid.
2. **Hero headline ≤ 10 words, road-sign legible at 1.5 meters.** Hard cap. No subtitle stacking to bypass the limit.
3. **Pricing tier table mandatory if product is sold at advertised price.** Optional only if the product is custom-quote ("Hubungi sales untuk penawaran") — in which case the pricing slot becomes a "How We Quote" page with named tier brackets.
4. **PPN 11% disclosure mandatory for Indonesian distribution.** Below pricing table: "Harga sudah termasuk PPN 11%." Failure to disclose = regulatory risk.
5. **No "Bicycle Bear" digital mimicry.** Print is fixed. Hover-state UI, expandable panels, and scroll-triggered hooks are anti-patterns. Use static information scaffolding.
6. **No banned vocabulary.** Global ban list applies. Additional brochure-product bans: "comprehensive", "end-to-end", "holistic", "seamless", "integrated platform" — replace with concrete module names and integration timelines.
