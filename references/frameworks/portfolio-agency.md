# Portfolio Agency — Brand-Consistent Multi-Case-Study Framework

## Purpose & When To Use This Framework

Use this framework when an agency (3+ practitioners) needs a print-ready or PDF-distributed portfolio that proves capability across multiple disciplines, consistent brand voice, and team depth. The audience is procurement, marketing director, brand manager, or founder evaluating the agency for a brand engagement, retainer, or RFP shortlist. Unlike portfolio-personal, this artifact must prove (a) the agency can deliver across multiple verticals/disciplines, (b) brand voice is consistent across team members, and (c) the engagement model is professional and scalable.

The differentiator from portfolio-personal: more case studies (3–5 featured, each 3 pages), a formal service catalog, an explicit team page, and process/methodology documentation. The narrative shifts from "here is my work" to "here is how we work for clients like you."

Default page count is 16 (cover + manifesto + services + 3 case studies × 3 pages + team + logos + process + contact). Compress to 12 for boutique agency with tight focus. Expand to 20 for full-service agency with multiple sector spreads.

## Narrative Arc / Page Sequence

1. **Page 1 — Cover.** Agency name + tagline + signature visual. Tagline is positioning, not poetry. Example: "Pentagram — Independent design partnership, 1972."
2. **Page 2 — Brand Manifesto (one page).** Why the agency exists, what it believes, what it refuses to do. Three to five sentences max. This is the soul of the deck — it sets the tone for the case studies that follow.
3. **Pages 3–4 — Service Capabilities Grid.** Two-page spread (or two facing single pages). Services grouped into 3–5 categories with sub-disciplines. Example for brand agency: Brand Strategy / Visual Identity / Digital Product / Editorial / Motion. Each service: one-line description + 2–4 sub-disciplines + one signature client logo.
4. **Pages 5–7 — Featured Case Study #1.** Three pages: **Brief & stakes** (1 page) → **Process & solution** (1 page) → **Outcome & client endorsement** (1 page). Hero project for the agency's flagship discipline. Outcome metrics are measured ("Brand consideration lifted from 12% to 34% in 18 months") or verbatim client endorsement with name + role.
5. **Pages 8–10 — Featured Case Study #2.** Same 3-page structure. Different discipline from Case Study #1 to prove range.
6. **Pages 11–13 — Featured Case Study #3.** Same 3-page structure. Third discipline. Three case studies prove range without overwhelming.
7. **Pages 14–15 — Team (1–2 pages).** Founders + senior practitioners. Per team member: photo, name, role, one-line credential ("Previously led brand at Stripe / Co-founded Y / Shipped X for Z"). Junior team optional, grouped if included.
8. **Page 16 — Client Logo Grid.** Single page of 12–24 client logos arranged in a uniform grid. Sorted by category or recency. No "various other clients" footnote — every logo earns its place.
9. **Page 17 — Process / Methodology.** One page diagramming how the agency works with clients. Example: Discover → Define → Design → Deliver, with named deliverables per phase and typical timeline. This is the "how do we engage with you" answer.
10. **Page 18 — Contact CTA.** Named partner contact (face photo, name, role, direct line, email), studio address, QR to scheduling tool. One CTA verb: "Start a project" / "Schedule a chemistry call" / "Submit an RFP."

## 7-Step Content Checklist

1. **Manifesto refuses something.** A manifesto that only embraces ("we believe in design") is wallpaper. A manifesto that refuses ("we do not pitch on spec; we do not accept work below Rp 500M; we do not work with tobacco or extractive industries") signals confidence and filter quality.
2. **Service grid uses agency-specific vocabulary.** "Brand Strategy" is generic. "Naming Sprints / Positioning Audits / Visual Identity Systems" is the agency's actual deliverable taxonomy. Per the framework-structures-2026 research, taxonomic specificity signals depth.
3. **Three case studies span three disciplines.** Hard rule. Three case studies in the same discipline says "we are a specialist agency" (which is fine, but then position as such on the cover and drop "agency" framing). Three across three proves range.
4. **Case study outcome page has measured number OR verbatim client quote with name + role.** Same rule as portfolio-personal §4, applied per case study.
5. **Team page credentials are shipping credentials.** "Previously at Google" is dead. "Led the redesign of [specific product] at [specific company]" is signal. One concrete win per senior team member.
6. **Client logo grid is uniform — no size hierarchy.** A grid where some logos are bigger than others signals "we are most proud of these clients." Uniform sizing signals "every client is a real engagement."
7. **Process page names deliverables per phase + typical timeline.** "Discovery phase" is vague. "Discovery phase: stakeholder interviews (8–12), competitive audit, positioning workshop. Typical: 3 weeks. Output: positioning brief + insight deck." Specific.

## Do-NOT Patterns (3+)

1. **"Our values" page that lists adjectives.** "Curious, collaborative, courageous" is generic. The manifesto carries the values implicitly through what the agency refuses to do.
2. **Founding story that takes 3+ pages.** Origin story is interesting to the founders, not the buyer. One paragraph in the manifesto or a single line on the team page. The case studies are the proof, not the origin story.
3. **Service grid with 12+ services.** Signals "we do everything," which signals "we specialize in nothing." Hard cap at 5 service categories; sub-disciplines bullet under each.
4. **Case studies that all use the same hero project across different disciplines.** Buyer notices and discounts the portfolio. Each case study is a different project + different client + different discipline.
5. **Team page with stock photos or missing photos.** Either real team photos at consistent treatment (B&W or consistent location) or no photos at all. Stock placeholder destroys trust instantly.
6. **Client logo grid with "and many more" overflow.** Either every claimed logo is on the grid, or the grid is honestly sized to what fits. Overflow framing reads insecure.
7. **"Comprehensive integrated full-service agency" anywhere.** Banned framing. Use specific service taxonomy and named client outcomes.

## Anti-Slop Hard Rules

1. **Brand voice consistency across team members.** Hard rule. If three case studies are written in three different voices (one playful, one corporate, one academic), the agency reads as fragmented. Editorial pass mandatory before validate stage.
2. **Every case study has measured outcome OR verbatim attributed client quote.** Same as portfolio-personal §1, applied per case study. Hard-fail if any case study ends in "client was happy."
3. **Visual ratio ≥ 70% per page, especially case study pages.** Hard rule.
4. **Three case studies span three disciplines.** Hard cap. More than 5 case studies dilutes; fewer than 3 fails the range-proof.
5. **Team photos at consistent treatment OR no team photos.** No mixed-treatment team grids.
6. **Client logo grid uniform sized.** No size hierarchy implying favorites.
7. **No banned vocabulary.** Global ban list applies. Additional portfolio-agency bans: "comprehensive", "integrated", "full-service", "end-to-end", "holistic", "results-driven agency", "passionate team" — replace with named service taxonomy + measured case outcomes + named team credentials.
