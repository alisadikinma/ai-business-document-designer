# Service Flyer — Single-Pulse 1–2 Page Framework

## Purpose & When To Use This Framework

Use this framework when the operator needs a 1- or 2-page A4 flyer for high-velocity distribution — door-drop, event handout, lobby placement, trade show booth, or QR-coded poster. The reader has under 5 seconds of attention. The flyer must communicate one value proposition and one call-to-action in that window, then earn a longer second read for the buyer who is already interested.

Per the framework-structures-2026 research backbone (§4 Service Flyer framework), this operates under the "Single-Pulse Principle" — one high-velocity message designed for the "Top-Third Eye Trajectory" (the print equivalent of "above-the-fold"). The headline acts as a road sign capturing attention within 1.5 seconds.

Default page count is 1 (single-sided A4). Use 2 pages (double-sided) when service tier articulation requires a tier comparison or when bilingual layout is mandatory.

## Narrative Arc / Page Sequence

1. **Top-Third Zone (Above the Fold) — Hero / Road-Sign Headline.** Headline + dominant visual fill the top third of the page. Headline is a road-sign: question, claim, or stake. Examples: "BANJIR? JANGAN PANIK." / "RAMADAN SALE — 50% OFF SEMUA PAKET." Visual is one image, not a collage.
2. **Middle Zone — Single Pulse Benefit + Service Tier (optional).** One paragraph (≤ 25 words) articulating the single benefit + optional tier table (single column, 2–3 tiers max). The middle zone is the "buyer-already-interested" zone — they have passed the road-sign test.
3. **Bottom Third — Primary CTA + Contact Block.** One CTA verb directed at one action. Contact block: phone (large, scannable), WhatsApp link/QR, address (if relevant), optional QR to landing page. Operating hours if applicable.

Page 2 (if used) carries: detailed tier comparison, testimonials with verbatim attribution + name + role, or the bilingual mirror of page 1. Page 2 never carries a competing CTA — same CTA verb, different scaffolding.

## 7-Step Content Checklist

1. **Road-sign headline ≤ 8 words.** Per the Single-Pulse Principle, the flyer headline cap is tighter than the brochure (≤ 10 words). Test: readable in 1.5 seconds from 2 meters. Use questions, numeric claims, or stake-in-the-ground statements.
2. **Single dominant hero visual fills top third.** No collage, no multi-image grid in the hero zone. One image, large, high-contrast against the headline.
3. **Single CTA verb on the page — no exceptions.** Per the research §4 do-not pattern: dual/competing CTAs is the dominant flyer failure mode. One verb, one destination. "Hubungi Sekarang" → one phone number. "Pesan Online" → one URL/QR. Not both.
4. **Tier articulation (if included) is single-column compressed.** Flyer tier table is 2 or 3 tiers maximum, each with 3–4 feature lines. No 7-feature Bronze/Silver/Gold matrix — that belongs in brochure-product. Tier on flyer is a teaser, not the full comparison.
5. **Contact block dominates the bottom third visually.** Phone number sized larger than body copy. WhatsApp QR or click-to-call link. The buyer who passes the road-sign test must convert in one finger-tap.
6. **Urgency frame is dated, not abstract.** "Promo berakhir 31 Mei 2026" beats "Limited time offer." If using urgency, the deadline is on the flyer in plain numerals.
7. **Bilingual layout (if mandatory) mirrors top-to-bottom, not side-by-side.** Page 1 Bahasa Indonesia, page 2 English mirror — or single page with bilingual headline pair stacked. Side-by-side bilingual on one A4 cuts the road-sign zone in half and fails the 1.5-second test.

## Do-NOT Patterns (3+)

1. **Dual/competing CTAs.** "Call us OR visit our website OR scan this QR" — the operator's most common flyer failure. The buyer with 5 seconds chooses none. Hard ban: one CTA verb.
2. **Generic stock imagery in the hero zone.** Stock handshakes, stock smiling diverse team, stock skyline-with-cyan-overlay — all fail the road-sign test. Use product photography, location photography, or commissioned illustration.
3. **Overcrowded margins / "information suffocation."** Per the research §4, dense margin packing makes the flyer feel cheap. Generous whitespace signals quality. Hard rule: outer margin minimum 12mm (15mm preferred) on A4.
4. **Headline longer than 8 words OR with subtitle stacking to bypass the cap.** Single-line headlines win the road-sign test. Subtitle stacking ("Premium Solutions for Modern Businesses — Transforming Industries Since 2010") doubles the cognitive load and fails the 1.5-second window.
5. **Phone number at footer-text size.** The phone number is the conversion point on most flyers. Size it visually large enough that a passerby can dial from the flyer at arm's length. Hard rule: phone number font-size ≥ 2× body copy.
6. **Tier table with 5+ tiers or 7+ feature rows.** Flyer compression discipline. Tier table on flyer is a teaser; the full comparison lives in the brochure or the landing page behind the QR code.

## Anti-Slop Hard Rules

1. **One page = one CTA verb.** Hard rule. Validate-fail on any second CTA verb anywhere on the flyer.
2. **Headline ≤ 8 words, road-sign legible at 2 meters.** Hard cap, tighter than brochure-product. No subtitle stacking.
3. **Outer margin ≥ 12mm.** Print bleed plus safe zone minimum. Hard rule per print-readiness validation.
4. **Phone number font-size ≥ 2× body copy.** Conversion-point sizing rule.
5. **Visual ratio ≥ 70%, with hero zone (top third) at 80%+ visual.** Single-pulse design is visual-led; copy scaffolds the visual.
6. **No banned vocabulary.** Global ban list applies. Additional service-flyer bans: "Premium solutions", "Modern businesses", "Transform your X", "Discover the difference" — replace with the specific service noun and the specific outcome.
