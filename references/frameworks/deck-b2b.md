# Deck B2B — Channel Partner / Operator Pitch Framework (Classic-10 Adapted)

## Purpose & When To Use This Framework

Use this framework when the operator is selling a deployment, pilot, or partnership to a B2B buyer who controls a venue, fleet, route, or distribution channel — and where the buyer cares about operator P&L, integration risk, and ROI payback months, not equity dilution. The audience is not a VC. The deck must answer: "If I deploy this in my venue / fleet / network, what is the cost, what is the uplift, when is payback, and what is your SLA?"

This is the Classic-10 sequence adapted for B2B sales motion. It is also the baseline for hybrid mode (B2B core + 3 VC appendix slides). Mode detection rules live in `references/storyline-frameworks.md`; the brief skill routes here when the audience signals adopt / deploy / pilot / venue / mall / EO / fleet / operator language.

## Narrative Arc / Page Sequence

1. **Title.** Product name + venue/partner logo lockup + one-line ROI promise. `[Product] for [Venue Class] — [Rp X uplift / N months payback]`. The venue logo signals "we already work with people like you."
2. **Operator Pain.** `[Operator class] loses Rp X / month to [specific cause]`. Visceral, specific, sized. Not "operators struggle with efficiency."
3. **Before / After.** The one image. Side-by-side: current state photo + after-state photo or screenshot. Transformation is shown, not described.
4. **Comparable Venues + GMV Uplift.** Named comparable venues (or operator classes) where the deployment is live, with measured GMV uplift in IDR per month. Logos + numbers. This is the proof slide.
5. **3-Pillar Product.** Three named modules that compose the deployment. Example: `POS module + Wristband + Control Room`. Three pillars, not five. If the system has five modules, group into three families.
6. **Operator P&L.** Cost line in IDR (one-time + monthly recurring) vs uplift line in IDR (GMV delta + cost saved) → payback in months. Show the math. No "ROI guaranteed."
7. **Yukk-Gap Matrix.** Feature gap matrix against the dominant comparable (Yukk for venue POS, competitor X for fleet, etc.). Honest competitive position — name the gaps you do not yet close and the gaps where you win.
8. **Team + SLA.** Founders + on-the-ground deployment team + 24/7 support contact + SLA in hours. The buyer needs to know who answers the phone at 2 AM during a venue event.
9. **Quarterly Value Roadmap.** Q1 / Q2 / Q3 named deliverables that compound the deployment's value. Not a feature list — an outcome list ("Q2: analytics agent reduces shrinkage 8%").
10. **Pilot Terms / Ask.** Scope (which venue, how many lanes/SKUs), commercial terms (one-time + monthly + revenue share if applicable), integration timeline (weeks to go-live), decision deadline. Specific.

Hybrid mode adds ≤3 VC appendix slides (financials, use of funds, cap table) when the channel-partner conversation also touches on equity.

## 7-Step Content Checklist

1. **Venue logo lockup on title.** Place a comparable venue's logo on the title slide. If none yet, place a representative venue category badge ("Mall Tier-1 EO" / "Fleet 50+ Truk"). Logo placement signals belonging.
2. **Pain slide is sized in IDR per month per venue.** Not aggregate. The buyer reads: "I lose Rp X per month." Otherwise pain is abstract and ignorable.
3. **GMV uplift is from a comparable, not a projection.** Use named pilot data. If no pilot yet, use the closest analog and label it "industry analog — pilot validation pending." Never fabricate.
4. **Operator P&L slide shows payback in months, not percent.** Buyers internalize months ("3 months payback") faster than percent ("400% ROI"). Months beats percent every time.
5. **Yukk-gap matrix is honest about losses.** A matrix where every cell is a checkmark for you signals you have not stress-tested the matrix. Buyer trust comes from naming 1–2 areas where the comparable still wins.
6. **SLA in hours, contact name on slide.** "24/7 support" is wallpaper. "<2hr response, escalation to [Name] [phone]" is a signal.
7. **Ask slide is a pilot proposal, not a sales close.** Scope + price + integration timeline + decision deadline. The buyer should be able to forward this slide to their CFO and procurement together.

## Do-NOT Patterns (3+)

1. **VC-deck pattern leakage.** "TAM SAM SOM" slides, "moat" slides, and "use of funds" slides belong in deck-vc, not here. The B2B operator does not care about your defensibility against foundation models — they care about their venue P&L.
2. **Generic before/after.** "Before: chaos / After: order" without specific photo or numeric delta is the most common B2B deck failure. Use actual venue photos with measured deltas.
3. **5-pillar / 7-pillar product slide.** Three pillars is the cognitive limit. More than three signals the product is unfocused, and the buyer will distrust the deployment plan.
4. **"ROI guaranteed" or "100% satisfaction."** Triggers procurement / legal flags. Replace with named pilot results and a sensitivity table in appendix.
5. **"Disrupting the industry" or "Revolutionizing operations."** Banned in deck-b2b because operators read this as outsider language. Use operator vocabulary: "reducing shrinkage", "lifting GMV per lane", "cutting reconciliation time."
6. **Founder bio walls.** "Stanford / Google / McKinsey" alone is dead in B2B. Show what each founder has deployed at scale in the same vertical. Domain experience > pedigree.

## Anti-Slop Hard Rules

1. **Operator P&L slide is non-negotiable.** No deck-b2b ships without a P&L slide showing IDR cost, IDR uplift, and payback in months. Validate-fail if absent or if numbers are unsourced.
2. **Pilot data or labeled analog.** Every claim of GMV uplift, cost saved, or efficiency gained is either (a) measured pilot data with venue name, or (b) labeled "industry analog — pilot validation pending." No unlabeled projections.
3. **Three pillars maximum on product slide.** Hard cap. Group if necessary. The cognitive limit is real and operator attention is the scarce resource.
4. **SLA in concrete hours and contact name.** "24/7 enterprise support" is meaningless. "<2hr response by [Named Engineer] at [Phone]" is the standard.
5. **No banned vocabulary.** Same global ban list — "Unlock", "Unleash", "Empower", "Supercharge", "Revolutionize", "Transform", "Game-changing", "Disrupt", "Synergize", "Leverage" (as verb), "Next-gen", "Cutting-edge", "State-of-the-art", "Paradigm-shift", "Best-in-class", "World-class". Plus B2B-specific bans: "End-to-end solution", "Seamless integration", "Holistic platform" (replace with concrete integration timeline and module names).
